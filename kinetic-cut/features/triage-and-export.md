# 3-Second Triage & Export

> Pick the slices you want and send them straight to your editing timeline — without ever duplicating your footage.

## What it does

After KineticCut analyses a video, it lays out the extracted ~3-second slices as a grid. You tap the ones you want, and KineticCut trims just those moments out of the original and hands them to the iOS share sheet — ready to drop into CapCut, Premiere, or anywhere else. Until you export, nothing is copied: each slice is just a lightweight pointer back into your original video.

## When to use it

- You've analysed a take and want to grab the best two or three moments.
- You're assembling B-roll and want clean, trimmed clips without re-encoding the whole video.
- You want to hand specific moments to another editing app.

## How it works

1. Analyse a video on the **Timeline** tab.
2. Open the **Export** tab — the extracted slices appear as a grid.
3. Tap the slices you want; the selection summary updates.
4. Tap **Export Selected to Timeline** and choose where to send the trimmed clips in the share sheet.

## Free vs Premium

- **Free:** select and export **one** slice at a time, at the standard 3-second length.
- **Pro Creator Pass:** export multiple slices at once (batch), and choose custom slice lengths (1s, 3s, or 5s).

## Privacy

Everything stays on device. Your slices are stored as lightweight time-pointers — never copies of your video — and a trimmed clip is created only at the moment you export. Nothing is uploaded or stored off-device.

## Known limits

- A slice points back into its source video; if you've ended the import session that provided it, export will report that the source is no longer available.
- Free exports are one clip at a time; batch export is a Pro feature.
- Exported trims are written to a temporary area and shared from there.
