# Scoped Media Intake

> Hand KineticCut just the videos you want it to look at — nothing else from your library.

## What it does

KineticCut never trawls your whole camera roll. Instead, you pick a small, bounded batch of videos — say, a single day's shoot — through Apple's system picker, and KineticCut works only with those. The selection opens a temporary session; when you're done, the videos are released and nothing lingers.

## When to use it

- You've just finished a shoot and want to triage that day's footage.
- You want a specific handful of clips analysed, without giving an app access to your entire photo library.
- You're privacy-conscious and want processing that never leaves your device.

## How it works

1. Open the **Clips** tab.
2. Tap **Browse Local Volumes**.
3. The system video picker appears — choose the videos you want (videos only; photos are hidden).
4. Your selection opens as a session and the chosen clips appear, ready for the next step.

## Free vs Premium

- **Free:** up to **3 videos** per session. Pick more and KineticCut shows an upgrade prompt instead.
- **Pro Creator Pass:** unlimited videos per batch.

## Privacy

Everything stays on device. KineticCut uses Apple's out-of-process picker, so it sees only the videos you explicitly choose — never the rest of your library — and asks for no broad photo-library permission. It does not collect, transmit, or persist any data off your device.

## Known limits

- Only video files are selectable; the picker filters out photos.
- A session is temporary by design — closing it releases the working copies of your clips.
- The picker draws from your Photos library; importing arbitrary files from the Files app isn't supported.
